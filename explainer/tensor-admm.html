Misconception checks (spec §21 — click to test yourself)

“A tensor is only a larger matrix.”No: axes here carry roles (s is translated, g/m/τ are spectators), units (person-trips), and lineage. The unfolding view shows the same tensor as three different matrices — arrangement is computational, the typed semantics are the mathematics.
“Every axis changes during a spatial translation.”Only s → z changes: 𝒴z,g,m,τ = Σs Bz,s 𝒳s,g,m,τ. Check the axis stack: g, m, τ ride along untouched. That is what spectator means.
“Preserving totals means no information is lost.”Press Demonstrate information loss: the fiber [7, 20, 15, 8] maps to exactly the same zone totals [27, 23] as [10, 14, 18, 8], because their difference (−3, 6, −3, 0) lies in the null space of B. Conservation of totals ≠ preservation of spatial detail — rank(B) = 2 < 4 source cells.
“ADMM is simple averaging.” Set the conflict scenario and watch the table: each module first solves its own penalized problem (closed form here), then the consensus averages locals plus duals and projects onto the feasible set, then duals absorb the disagreement. The final z = 17.532 is the weight-adjusted compromise (Σw·a/Σw), not the plain mean 17.567.
“ADMM convergence guarantees transportation feasibility.”Convergence certifies agreement, not validity: if the modules agree on a value that violates a capacity or a conserved total not encoded in Ω, the residuals go to zero anyway. That is why the Conservation tab is a separate certificate from the ADMM tab — and why the diagnostic label can read “converged but physically invalid.”
